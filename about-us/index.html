When you solve a puzzle

When you solve a puzzle, your brain is actively engaged in problem-solving and decision-making. This process involves several areas of the brain, including the prefrontal cortex, which is responsible for planning and decision-making, and the temporal lobes, which are involved in memory and perception. As you work on a puzzle, your brain is constantly processing and integrating information from your senses, as well as recalling and using stored information from your memory. This process of actively thinking and problem-solving can be mentally stimulating and can help to keep your brain active and healthy. Solving puzzles can also activate the release of neurotransmitters, such as dopamine and serotonin, which are associated with feelings of pleasure and satisfaction. This can contribute to the feeling of accomplishment and enjoyment that many people experience when they solve a puzzle.



Our Mission

Our screen time time has seen an upward because of  Multiple Cartoon Channel, Mobile phones, Gaming, YouTube, Social Media, Binge watch on OTT and many more.

Our IQ level is also coming down because people dont tend to remember things, they can either do google search or ask there favourite voice assistant like Google/Alexa/Siri.There are advantages to it but at the same time kids are notbuilding memory muscles and neuron connections.
